# Figures: the results story

Rendered by `python -m scripts.make_figures` from the committed measurement JSON in [`../results/`](../results/) (regenerate that data with `scripts/run_measurements.sh`, which trains the multi-agent pool too; the standalone variance/exploitability/tilt results have their own measure scripts, see [../GUIDE.md](../GUIDE.md)). Each `.png` is for the write-up; the matching `.html` is interactive.

**Start with [`exec_summary.png`](exec_summary.png)**: every headline edge with its 95% bootstrap CI (the honest 'are the edges real?' view). Narrative: [../THESIS.md](../THESIS.md). Bibliography: [../REFERENCES.md](../REFERENCES.md).

| [`exploitability.png`](exploitability.png) | §rigor | Exact Leduc exploitability (NashConv; 0 = exact Nash). The CFR time-average converges (0.695 to 0.0094), but the greedy last-iterate stays exploitable (~2.221) and does not converge. A tabular Q-learning self-play (a tabular analog of the DQN regime) shows the same pattern: its greedy last-iterate oscillates around 3.40 (range [1.70, 5.53] over 1M+ episodes) and never approaches Nash, genuine non-convergence of the greedy iterate, not a CFR artifact. Adding only policy-averaging to that same Q-learner (tabular NFSP) flips the outcome: its average policy's exploitability falls 2.40 to 0.86 over 50k to 1000k episodes. This is the rigorous reason DQN self-play does not reach Nash while averaging methods do (REFERENCES.md S1). |

The 95% CI excludes 0: a statistically resolved edge over the baseline (this is the exploratory pilot; the 50-seed training monitor is noisy, significantly negative mid-training at 15/50 at step 500 before recovering to 33/50 at the final checkpoint, so the resolved claim rests on the 200-seed pilot and the separate pre-registered confirmatory at 500 mirrored seeds, which pins it to +256 [+144, +364]). |

| [`tilt_realdata.png`](tilt_realdata.png) | §real-data | Real-data tilt validation (12,320 sessions, 777,687 hand-rows from 120 PokerStars 25NL files; PHH/Kim 2024, CC-BY-4.0, used for opponent-model validation only). After a >='10bb loss, 873 real 2009 online players play looser (VPIP +2.8pp) and more aggressively (+1.6pp): both 95% CIs exclude 0. The project's forward-filter HMM detector registers a resolved P(tilted) separation (+0.011, CI excludes 0). Shuffled-label placebo collapses to ~0 for each effect. A separate Baum-Welch HMM corroborates the phenomenon: it beats a 1-state model out-of-sample (held-out LL +681; held-out DBIC +1315) with a calm (P[aggr]=0.12) and an active (P[aggr]=0.30) regime, with the active regime 1.9x enriched for a recent big loss (4.3% vs 2.3% base). |

The matched effect is larger than post-loss-vs-baseline because players also tighten after a win. |
