# Figures — the results story

Rendered by `python -m scripts.make_figures` from the committed measurement JSON in [`../results/`](../results/) (regenerate that data with `scripts/run_measurements.sh`, which trains the §10 pool too; the standalone variance/exploitability/tilt results have their own measure scripts — see [../GUIDE.md](../GUIDE.md)). Each `.png` is for the write-up; the matching `.html` is interactive.

**Start with [`exec_summary.png`](exec_summary.png)** — every headline edge with its 95% bootstrap CI (the honest "are the edges real?" view). Narrative: [../THESIS.md](../THESIS.md). Bibliography: [../references.md](../references.md).

| [`tilt_realdata.png`](tilt_realdata.png) | §real-data | Real-data tilt validation (12,320 sessions, 777,687 hand-rows from 120 PokerStars 25NL files; PHH/Kim 2024, CC-BY-4.0 — used for OPPONENT-MODEL validation ONLY, never the policy). After a ≥10bb loss, 873 real 2009 online players play looser (VPIP +2.8pp) and more aggressively (rate +1.6pp) — both 95% CIs exclude 0 — and the project's emission-only forward-filter HMM detector (tilted-state emission means μ_normal=0.11, μ_tilted=0.643 fixed from the population before measuring, not tuned) registers a small but resolved P(tilted) separation (+0.011, CI excludes 0); each effect's shuffled-label placebo collapses to ~0 (gray). A separate Baum-Welch HMM (distinct from the forward-filter detector) corroborates the phenomenon — it beats a 1-state model out-of-sample (held-out LL +681; held-out ΔBIC +1315) with a calm (P[aggr]=0.12) and an active (P[aggr]=0.30) regime, and the active regime is 1.9× enriched for a recent big loss (4.3% vs 2.3% base). Honest scale: the shifts are small (1-3pp) — real but marginal, the project's signature. This predictable, post-loss deviation is the poker analog of the adverse-selection signal of references.md §3 (Kyle; Glosten-Milgrom) — the cross-domain mapping to real order flow remains an untested hypothesis; the human-vs-bot contrast is corroborated by Haaf et al. 2021 (§6). |
