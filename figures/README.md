Rendered by `python -m scripts.make_figures` from the committed measurement JSON in [`../results/`](../results/) (regenerate that data with `scripts/run_measurements.sh`, which trains the multi-agent pool too; the standalone variance/exploitability/tilt results have their own measure scripts — see [../GUIDE.md](../GUIDE.md)). **Start with [`exec_summary.png`](exec_summary.png)** — every headline edge with its 95% bootstrap CI (the honest "are the edges real?" view). Narrative: [../THESIS.md](../THESIS.md). Bibliography: [../REFERENCES.md](../REFERENCES.md).

Adding ONLY policy-averaging to that same Q-learner (tabular NFSP) flips the outcome: its AVERAGE policy's exploitability falls 2.40 → 0.86 over 50k → 1000k episodes — the learned averaging method converging on the same exact metric (sample-based, so above CFR's full-enumeration limit). This is the rigorous, exact reason DQN self-play does not reach Nash while averaging methods do (CFR here; NFSP carries the same averaging to a learned policy — REFERENCES.md §1). |

| [`tilt_realdata.png`](tilt_realdata.png) | §real-data | Real-data tilt validation (12,320 sessions, 777,687 hand-rows from 120 PokerStars 25NL files; PHH/Kim 2024, CC-BY-4.0 — used for OPPONENT-MODEL validation ONLY, never the policy). After a ≥10bb loss, 873 real 2009 online players play looser (VPIP +2.8pp) and more aggressively (rate +1.6pp) — both 95% CIs exclude 0 — and the project's emission-only forward-filter HMM detector (tilted-state emission means μ_normal=0.11, μ_tilted=0.643 fixed from the population before measuring, not tuned) registers a small but resolved P(tilted) separation (+0.011, CI excludes 0); each effect's shuffled-label placebo collapses to ~0 (gray). A separate Baum-Welch HMM (distinct from the forward-filter detector) corroborates the phenomenon — it beats a 1-state model out-of-sample (held-out LL +681; held-out ΔBIC +1315) with a calm (P[aggr]=0.12) and an active (P[aggr]=0.30) regime, and the active regime is 1.9× enriched for a recent big loss (4.3% vs 2.3% base). Honest scale: the shifts are small (1-3pp) in absolute size but statistically resolved (both CIs exclude 0) — one of the project's two real-data headline findings. This predictable, post-loss deviation is the poker analog of the adverse-selection signal of REFERENCES.md §3 (Kyle; Glosten-Milgrom) — the cross-domain mapping to real order flow remains an untested hypothesis; the human-vs-bot contrast is corroborated by Haaf et al. 2021 (REFERENCES.md §6). |
| [`tilt_lossvswin.png`](tilt_lossvswin.png) | §real-data | Symmetric within-player control (the confound-controlled tilt test). The post-loss-vs-baseline shift can be confounded — looser players both lose more and play looser — so compare each player's hand after a ≥10bb LOSS against their hand after an equal ≥10bb WIN, matching player, big-pot arousal, and event size so only the swing SIGN differs. After a loss players are +3.6pp more aggressive and +2.9pp looser than after an equal win (95% CIs exclude 0; Cohen d=0.25/0.14; n=685 matched players); the shuffled-label placebo collapses to ~0. The matched effect is LARGER than post-loss-vs-baseline because players also tighten after a win — a clean prospect-theory loss-aversion asymmetry, not generic big-pot arousal. |
